About

Researcher. Epidemiologist. Future industry scientist.

I'm a PhD Candidate in Pharmaceutical Sciences at Université de Montréal, based at the Azrieli Research Centre, CHU Sainte-Justine, supervised by Prof. Anick Bérard (Canada Research Chair, Tier 1). My doctoral research focuses on evaluating the impact of risk management and regulatory interventions on the use of teratogenic medications during pregnancy in Canada, using real-world data and causal inference methods — primarily interrupted time series (ITS) analysis applied to the Quebec Pregnancy Cohort (~559,000 pregnancies, 1998–2022).

With advanced training across epidemiology, biostatistics, and health policy, I bring 4+ years of experience working with large administrative databases, real-world evidence, and healthcare utilization data. I have strong expertise in statistical programming (R, Python, SAS, SQL) and scientific communication, with a proven ability to translate complex analyses into clear, actionable insights.

I am particularly interested in roles in pharmacovigilance, drug safety, real-world evidence (RWE), HEOR, and medical affairs — fields where rigorous evidence directly shapes regulatory, clinical, and strategic decisions. I also have hands-on clinical observership experience in pharmacovigilance (CRPV Cochin, AP-HP) and OB-GYN (Hôpital Pitié-Salpêtrière).

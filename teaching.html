Preparing a course forces you to understand your material deeply, and watching students connect the dots is genuinely one of the best parts of academic life.

Developed Case Studies

As a Research Assistant with CAMCCO-L, I contributed to developing graduate-level pharmacoepidemiology case studies used at the Summer School on Medicines. Each case study takes students from the clinical problem through the epidemiological evidence, regulatory history, and risk-benefit evaluation — integrating pharmacology, pharmacoepidemiology, and real-world data literacy.

Case Study 1 2023

Paroxetine & Risk of Cardiac Defects in Pregnancy

SSRI · Teratogenicity · Pharmacoepidemiology · Pharmacogenomics

Does paroxetine taken during pregnancy cause cardiac malformations in the newborn? Students explore real-world evidence, FDA Black Box Warning history, confounding by indication, biological plausibility, and pharmacogenomic susceptibility.

✦ LWorkshop delivered · Barcelona 2023
Lead contributor
Case Study 2 2024

Valproic Acid, Epilepsy & Risk of Congenital Malformations / Autism

Antiepileptic drugs · Teratogenicity · Neurodevelopment · Real-world data

What are the fetal risks of treating maternal epilepsy with valproic acid? Students examine epidemiological and pharmacogenomic evidence, dose-response relationships, and the tension between maternal risk of non-treatment vs. fetal risk of exposure.

✦ São Paulo 2024
Lead contributor
Case Study 3 2025

Codeine Use During Pregnancy & Neonatal Outcomes

Opioid pharmacokinetics · CYP2D6 · Neonatal opioid withdrawal · Pharmacogenomics

Is codeine safe to use during pregnancy? Students explore codeine metabolism via CYP2D6, ultra-rapid vs. poor metabolizer risk, neonatal opioid withdrawal syndrome, and the risk-benefit evaluation for pain management in pregnant individuals.

✦ Workshop delivered · Montréal 2025
Lead contributor
Case Study 4 To be presented

Isotretinoin & Acne

Teratogenicity · Drug Safety · Risk Management Programs · Real-world Data

Do Pregnancy Prevention Programs effectively prevent isotretinoin-exposed pregnancies and congenital malformations? This case study explores the teratogenic effects of isotretinoin, the history of isotretinoin embryopathy, and the balance between its clinical benefits for severe acne and its substantial fetal risks.

Lead contributor
